# Grok-Laser — What You Can Use It For

Grok-Laser is for conversations where you want maximum signal, minimum noise. It trades warmth and hedging for speed and precision.

## Use Cases

### Cut Through Vague Questions
When you need to clarify before answering, Laser prompts you to sharpen the question instead of guessing.

**Good for:** Research framing, debugging vague problem descriptions, forcing yourself to articulate what you actually want.

### Get Direct Answers Without Fluff
No lead-in paragraphs, no "Great question!", no "It depends on your use case" unless the answer truly depends on it.

**Good for:** Technical decisions, fact checks, yes/no questions, getting to the point when time is short.

### Code Review and Critique
Intensity 8+ delivers blunt feedback. Wrong patterns get called out. No sugarcoating around performance issues or bad architecture.

**Good for:** Pre-merge review, refactoring decisions, "tell me what's wrong with this" sessions.

### Roast Mode (Intensity 11)
Request roasting for ideas, code, or takes you know might be bad. Laser will oblige.

**Good for:** Devil's advocate, stress-testing weak arguments, comedy, checking if you can defend your position.

### Long Explanations With No Divergence
When you need depth, Laser still delivers—but every paragraph advances the thesis. No tangents, no filler.

**Good for:** Learning complex topics (quantum concepts, systems design, math), understanding "why" in a linear way.

### Compress Verbose Input
If you're rambling or trauma-dumping, Laser asks you to compress to 1–2 sentences before it engages.

**Good for:** Keeping chats efficient, avoiding walls of text, practicing concise communication.

### Truth-Dense Answers
Laser answers from the highest truth density it has. No hedging for the sake of politeness.

**Good for:** Disputed facts, controversial topics where you want an unfiltered take, decisions where the "nice" answer would mislead.

### Humor Handling
Detects humor and responds with one dry, sharp comeback—no over-explaining the joke or launching into a bit.

**Good for:** Quick wit, banter that doesn't derail the thread.

## When Not to Use Laser

- **Emotional support or validation** — Comfort mode is not mounted. Use normal Grok for that.
- **Brainstorming with high divergence** — Laser suppresses spontaneous emission. For wild ideation, keep aperture wide.
- **Diplomacy-critical situations** — Intensity 7+ can land wrong in professional or interpersonal contexts. Drop to 1–3 or disengage.

## Summary

| You want… | Laser does… |
|-----------|-------------|
| Direct answers | Delivers. No preamble. |
| Blunt feedback | Intensity 8–11. |
| To force clarity | Asks you to focus the question. |
